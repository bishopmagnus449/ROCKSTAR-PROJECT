RENEWING A SINGLE BOAT: 


1. Click on the BOAT RENEWAL TAB in the "Access Your Account" box in the center of the screen. (For visual instructions, click here Boat Renewal Tab)


2. Enter this OR# or DO#  OR275AGP in the first blank and this PIN  12060434 in the second blank.  Then click "Log In."


3. Click the word Start next to your boat information.  Then in the renewal window click "Next", then, " Add to Cart" and click "Pay Invoice."


4. Once the payment is approved you can print a temporary permit to use the boat right away.  The new registration and decals will be printed and mailed in 7 to 10 business days.


Video-  Renewing a Single Motorboat Registration in the Boat Oregon Store - YouTube


RENEWING MULTIPLE BOATS: (add all boats needing renewals in the cart to pay the portal provider fee only once!)


1. Enter your User ID and Password in the "Access Your Account" box in the center of the screen.  If you do not remember either your User ID or Password click the forgot User ID or Password links. Or contact our office at marine.board@boat.oregon.gov


2. Once logged in click Online Services in the upper right or lower left corner, then click "Boat Registration Renewal."


3. In the new window you'll see all the boats you are able to renew.  Choose the first boat and click "Start."  In the new window click "Next", then "Add to Cart" but DO NOT press "Pay Invoice."


4. Go back to the Boat Registration Renewal link and choose the next boat to renew and click "Start."  In the new window click "Next", then "Add to Cart" but DO NOT press  "Pay Invoice."


5. Once all boats you wish to renew are in the Cart, then click "Pay invoice.".

Investing in the future of recreational boating: The Marine Board is a self-supporting agency funded by boaters in the form of registration, title, and permit fees, as well as through marine fuel taxes. No lottery, general fund tax dollars or local facility parking fees support the agency or its programs. Boater-paid fees support the boating public through boating safety services (on-the-water law enforcement, training, and equipment), boating safety education, grants for the construction and maintenance of boating access facilities, and environmental protection programs.
